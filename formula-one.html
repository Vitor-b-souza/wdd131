Understanding Formula 1

Race Format

F1 races typically last approximately 2 hours. Drivers compete over a set number of laps on various circuits around the world. The driver who crosses the finish line first wins the race and gains valuable championship points.

Points System

Drivers earn points based on their finishing position. The top 10 finishers receive points, with first place earning 25 points. These points accumulate throughout the season to determine the Driver's Champion and the Constructor's Champion.

Teams & Manufacturers

Each team fields two drivers and operates with a crew of engineers, mechanics, and strategists. The car's design, setup, and technology are crucial to competitive success. Manufacturers compete for the Constructor's Championship.

Safety & Regulations

Formula 1 maintains strict technical regulations to ensure fair competition and driver safety. The FIA continuously updates these regulations to promote exciting racing while protecting competitors from harm.
