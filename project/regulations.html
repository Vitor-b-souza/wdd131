Formula 1 Regulations 2025

Technical and sporting rules for the 2025 season

Technical Regulations

Flexi-Wing Restrictions New ▼
  • DRS Deployment Modes: The rear wing must only have two positions (closed and open), eliminating the "mini-DRS" concept used by teams.
  • Rear Wing Deflection: The mainplane cannot flex more than 6mm under load, while the upper flap cannot move more than 7mm when load is applied horizontally.
  • Slot Gap Control: The gap between the mainplane and rear-wing flap is restricted to strict measurements, reducing variable deflection during the race.
  • Front Wing Deflection: Additional front wing tests reduce deflection from 10mm to 5mm under specified load conditions.
Driver Cooling Systems New ▼
  • Mandatory in Extreme Heat: When FIA predicts ambient temperatures over 30.5°C, teams must fit driver cooling systems.
  • System Components: Includes a fireproof cooling vest with 48m of piping containing cooling liquid, pump, plumbing, and thermal storage.
  • Weight Allowance: Cars gain an additional 5kg weight allowance when cooling systems are mandated.
  • Coolant Specifications: Coolant must be air, water, or aqueous solution of sodium chloride, potassium chloride, or propylene glycol.
Minimum Weight and Driver Allowance Updated ▼
  • Car Minimum Weight: The overall minimum weight limit (excluding fuel) increased from 798kg to 800kg.
  • Driver Weight Allowance: The minimum driver weight allowance increased from 80kg to 82kg to support taller drivers.
  • Car Plus Driver Total: The combined minimum weight of car and driver is now 800kg.
  • Heat Hazard Mass Increase: An additional 5kg is allowed when cooling systems are mandated due to heat conditions.
Gearbox Unlimited Removed ▼
  • No Gearbox Limit: There is no longer a limit on the number of gearboxes used by teams during the season.
  • Reliability Achievement: Modern gearbox designs have proven sufficiently reliable, eliminating the need for usage restrictions.
  • Grid Penalty Exemption: Exceeding gearbox quotas no longer results in grid penalties.

Sporting Regulations

Fastest Lap Point Removed Changed ▼
  • Point Elimination: The one-point bonus for fastest lap (awarded to drivers finishing in top 10) has been completely removed.
  • Reason for Change: Teams were making controversial late-race pit stops for fresh tires to secure the point, sometimes taking drivers out of points contention.
  • Impact: Maximum points available from a Grand Prix reduced from 26 to 25 for the winner.
  • Historical Note: Fastest lap points were originally part of F1 from 1950-1959 before being reintroduced in 2019.
Tire Usage and Strategy Updated ▼
  • Tire Lap Limit: Each set of tires is limited to 25 laps total across the entire race weekend (can be split across sessions).
  • Two-Stop Minimum: At Monaco, all drivers must make a minimum of two pit stops to use different tire compounds.
  • Three Compound Requirement: Teams must use at least three sets of tires in races, with minimum two different compounds for dry races.
  • Wet Weather Exception: In fully wet conditions, drivers may use only full wet tires per FIA mandate.
Safety Car Tire Rules New ▼
  • Optional Full Wets: Teams are no longer forced to fit full wet tyres for wet safety car starts; they may choose intermediate tyres.
  • FIA Override Authority: The FIA retains flexibility to mandate full wet tyres if deemed necessary for safety.
  • Weather Assessment: Teams can now make tactical decisions based on track conditions at safety car starts.
Qualifying in Poor Visibility New ▼
  • Weather-Based Grid: If qualifying cannot be held due to poor visibility, the grid is set by championship classification.
  • First Race Exception: At the season opener, stewards have discretion to determine the grid order if championship classification cannot be used.
  • Sprint Format: Same rules apply if a Sprint Qualifying session cannot take place.
Pit Lane Start Procedures Updated ▼
  • Ordered Departure: Once all cars have passed pit exit, pit lane starters must depart in prescribed order.
  • Formation Lap Requirement: Pit lane starters must re-enter the pit lane at the end of the formation lap before the race proper begins.
  • Consistent Procedure: This establishes more predictable and consistent pre-race procedures.
Rookie Testing Opportunities Expanded ▼
  • Doubled FP1 Sessions: Mandatory FP1 session time for rookies has doubled from one to two sessions per car.
  • Team Allocation: With two cars per team, this provides four occasions per team to run a rookie driver.
  • Rookie Definition: A rookie is defined as a driver who has completed not more than two Grands Prix.
  • Driver Development: The expansion supports better preparation for young drivers entering their first F1 season.
