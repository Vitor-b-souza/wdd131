Formula 1 Website Plan

Site Name

Formula 1 - Official Racing Championship Hub

Domain: formula1-championship.com

Logo Description: The iconic "F1" emblem with the number one integrated into a dynamic, flowing design representing speed and motion.

Rationale: This name clearly identifies the site as the central hub for Formula 1 racing content. It emphasizes the championship aspect while remaining professional and direct. The domain choice reflects the global nature of F1 while being memorable and easy to type.

Site Purpose

The Formula 1 website serves as the primary digital destination for racing enthusiasts, providing comprehensive coverage of the world's most prestigious motorsport championship. The site delivers:

  • Real-time race news and updates from all Grand Prix events
  • Detailed profiles of current drivers and legendary Hall of Fame competitors
  • Information about iconic and contemporary racing circuits
  • Current technical and sporting regulations with simplified explanations for newcomers
  • Visual galleries showcasing each team's current vehicle designs
  • A comprehensive beginner's guide to Formula 1 racing

User Scenarios

  1. "I'm new to Formula 1 - where do I start?" - A new fan can access the "Rules for Beginners" section to understand basic race mechanics, point scoring, and circuit types, enabling them to follow the sport comprehensively.
  2. "Which drivers are competing in 2025 and which teams are they with?" - The current drivers page displays the 2025 grid with team affiliations, allowing visitors to understand the current competitive landscape including recent driver transfers like Lewis Hamilton to Ferrari.
  3. "What are the legendary drivers of Formula 1?" - The Hall of Fame section showcases iconic drivers like Ayrton Senna, Michael Schumacher, and Alain Prost with their achievements and records.
  4. "Where are the 2025 races taking place?" - The circuits page displays all 24 venues on the 2025 calendar with details about iconic tracks like Monaco, Silverstone, and Monza.
  5. "How do the 2025 regulation changes affect the sport?" - The regulations section explains new technical rules for 2025 including flexi-wing restrictions and driver cooling systems.
  6. "What do the new team cars look like for 2025?" - The team galleries page provides visual representations of each constructor's current vehicles.

Color Schema

Racing Red (#FF1801)

Usage: Primary accent color for headings, call-to-action buttons, hover states on navigation, and highlighting important information. Represents the passion and energy of Formula 1 racing.

Deep Black (#15151E)

Usage: Primary background color for the site and text containers. Creates high contrast for readability and provides a sophisticated, professional appearance.

White (#FFFFFF)

Usage: Primary text color for body content and supporting text. Ensures excellent contrast against the dark background for accessibility and readability.

Dark Gray (#404048)

Usage: Secondary background color for cards, navigation bars, and content containers. Provides visual hierarchy and breaks up the pure black background.

Implementation Notes

This site plan establishes the foundation for a comprehensive Formula 1 website. The design prioritizes:

  • Responsive layout that adapts to mobile, tablet, and desktop screens
  • High contrast color scheme ensuring accessibility compliance
  • Clear navigation structure supporting both new and experienced F1 fans
  • Performance optimization through lazy-loaded images and efficient CSS
  • Dynamic content management using JavaScript for interactive elements
