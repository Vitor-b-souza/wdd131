Hall of Fame

Legendary drivers who defined Formula 1

Seven-Time World Champions

Lewis Hamilton
British | 2007-2024
7x Champion 103 Wins
World Championships 7
Career Wins 103
Pole Positions 103
Fastest Laps 52
Lewis Hamilton holds the record for most career wins and pole positions in Formula 1 history. His seven world championships tie him with Michael Schumacher for the most titles ever. A dominant force throughout his career with Mercedes and Ferrari.
Michael Schumacher
German | 1991-2006
7x Champion 91 Wins
World Championships 7
Career Wins 91
Pole Positions 68
Fastest Laps 41
Michael Schumacher dominated Formula 1 with Ferrari, winning five consecutive world championships from 2000-2004. His precision, consistency, and fitness redefined what it meant to be an elite F1 competitor. One of the greatest drivers of all time.

Four-Time World Champions

Max Verstappen
Dutch | 2015-Present
4x Champion 63 Wins
World Championships 4
Career Wins 63
Pole Positions 37
Max Verstappen emerged as one of F1's most dominant forces, winning four consecutive World Championships. His aggressive driving style and incredible racecraft have cemented him as one of the greatest drivers of his generation.
Alain Prost
French | 1980-1993
4x Champion 51 Wins
World Championships 4
Career Wins 51
Pole Positions 33
Nicknamed "The Professor" for his calculated and intelligent driving style, Alain Prost dominated the 1980s with fierce rivalries with Ayrton Senna. His consistency and precision made him one of the greatest tacticians in Formula 1 history.
Sebastian Vettel
German | 2007-2022
4x Champion 53 Wins
World Championships 4
Career Wins 53
Pole Positions 57
Sebastian Vettel won four consecutive World Championships with Red Bull from 2010-2013, showcasing his aggressive racecraft and focus. His dominance with 9 consecutive wins in 2013 remains one of F1's most impressive achievements.

Three-Time World Champions

Ayrton Senna
Brazilian | 1984-1994
3x Champion 41 Wins
World Championships 3
Career Wins 41
Pole Positions 65
One of motorsport's most legendary figures, Ayrton Senna remains the benchmark for raw talent and charisma. His legendary wet-weather performances and incredible qualifying pace made him a fearless competitor. His tragic death at the 1994 San Marino Grand Prix deeply affected the sport.
Niki Lauda
Austrian | 1972-1985
3x Champion 25 Wins
World Championships 3
Career Wins 25
Pole Positions 24
Niki Lauda's remarkable comeback from a life-threatening crash at the Nurburgring to win his second championship in 1977 remains one of F1's greatest stories. His third championship in 1984 and later business acumen cemented his legendary status.

Two-Time World Champions

Fernando Alonso
Spanish | 2003-Present
2x Champion 32 Wins
World Championships 2
Career Wins 32
Pole Positions 32
Fernando Alonso broke Michael Schumacher's dominance by winning back-to-back championships in 2005-2006 at age 25. He remains one of the most naturally gifted drivers in F1 history, known for his exceptional racecraft and defensive driving abilities.

Other Legends

Juan Manuel Fangio
Argentine | 1950-1958
5x Champion 24 Wins
World Championships 5
Career Wins 24
Pole Positions 29
Juan Manuel Fangio's ability to adapt to different teams and cars throughout his career was extraordinary. His five world championships were considered an untouchable record for decades, and he remains a motorsport legend.
